Mario and Bowser: Frenemies Forever/ New Frenemy Adventure/ Redux

How close is too close? That's what we exlore here.

"Mario and Bowser: Frenemies Forever": You know what they say, keep your friends close and your enemies closer! Will Mario and Bowser's associates approve? Was the illness that disrupted their tennis match part of something bigger? ... Other genres: Drama, humor

My signature project from early 2016 through late 2017, my second submission to FF.net, and my first work written specifically for the site. Loosely inspired by an old offline handwritten comic of mine called 'J-Man and the Dragon' and EchoEternal's 'Eternally Never Yours' (or specifically the first few chapters of that), this story was dedicated to Mario and Bowser's intense rivalry and against expectations their surprising camaraderie. Without going too deep into the plot it was definitely a rocky submission and made up as I went, yet also one of my most popular stories and as you will see, an idea I could never quite let go. The FF.net hyperlink is preserved above, but below you can find it without ads or leaving the site. Note that this is taken directly from FF.net. I uploaded portions of this on Wattpad and A03 but never formatted it right or completed the transfer. Also note that any typos etc are preserved.

  • Chapters 1-5, Chapters 6-13, Chapters 14-23, Chapters 24-28, Unused snippets, previously shared on tumblr here!
Click for more of my observations (may contain spoilers)
  • I regard this fic better now than I did after completion in 2017, where all I could notice were plot holes, tone shifts, and other issues. I can appreciate this today as an off the cuff yet earnest effort. As expounded in supplementary material like the Tropes doc, the real plot here involves how Mario and Bowser's friends react to the stunning revelation that they are 'friends', while the titular duo get separated after a few chapters. Not technically a mistake, just a consequence of me making this up as I went. I do try to make characters dynamic and interesting and I believe I succeed on some level, despite this being my first time writing most of them. It's not trying to hard to be funny nor get over its head with a cosmic conflict. (See the spoilers of the next story). If I could tighten up parts and cut the chatter, I'd rate this just behind Redux. When FF1 gains momentum I think it's a great story with a bookend-like conclusion that is far more natural than it's sequels. The two epilogues are clunkily me trying to patch up loose ends of course, but ultimately ambiguity remains over certain bits, the better choice compared to again some observations you'll see below.

"Mario and Bowser's New Frenemy Adventure": (Link removed, goes to hosted version)Mario and friends (and enemies) experience love, hate, and everything in between as they learn that sometimes the true villains aren't the ones fought in the games... Other Genres: Humor/ Suspense (T rating for later chapters)

A direct response to some of what I considered to be the largest issues of Frenemies Forever, including the fact that Mario and Bowser don't get to work together for long. It keeps at first the same premise, direct recreations of FF1 chapters, and even has the same villains, but then spirals considerably with a newly introduced foe that cannot be defeated by mere mortals! 'NFA' is split into two 'seasons', a setup and aftermath with an intentional tone shift. The stakes are always rising in this one, and there are more character interactions and jokes than ever. It was completed entirely in the year of 2018 and so planned in advance that I have a plethora of unused material and alternate directions linked below. Notably it also signified a shift into more mature themes with and more T rated content. There's some cool moments all throughout the story, but if you're not already a fan of the series, I'd almost recommend you stick with FF1 and then Redux much later for a few reasons. See the spoilers for the straight dope.

Click for more of my observations (may contain spoilers)
  • I grew a lot from this project, what I learned for a certainty making my later works much more focused and restrained- just overall better. For 'NFA' unfortunately I struggled with ambivalence on direction. The end result is coherent, but from being cobbled from various ideals meant to be more than they are, it's not balanced. There is a plot, and unlike FF1 things don't just randomly get introduced, but antic filled sequences take the forefront often. Returning characters were exaggerated to various degrees for the sake of comedy. At the same time when trying to flesh them out, I did so via some slower interlude like moments to mixed feedback, plus sometimes we were doing that with OCs, which will also be ymmv. Now going back to the opener, the voices I will still use now for most Mario characters came from here. There are a lot of cool things going on in this fic, but there for the sake of being there. It's like a fan fic of a fan fic, not the easiest sale when FF2 below and Redux put their story first and instead squeeze the funny bits, antics, and banter in between the gaps left.

"Mario and Bowser: Frenemies Forever Redux" : Linked on FF.net, but also on A03, from 2022-23. The rewrite of FF2, replacing it on the internet, and giving us a more mature and nuanced take on Mario and Bowser's relationship. I think the summary is all you need:

"As the adage goes, 'keep your friends close and your enemies closer'!
But.. What if, despite yourself, the enemy was too close? When the Mushroom Kingdom to Dark Land Peace Pact is insidiously broken, the stakes become so high that everyone's secrets, even those buried deepest must come out.

Click for more of my observations (global), (may contain spoilers)
  • The original 2016 Frenemies story was created during a very uncertain and transitional period in life, manifesting in a tale that shifts from jokey to serious. For New Frenemy Adventure in 2018, life was more stable and it showed with me being creative and experimental. The saccharine conclusion and flanderized characters striped some of the depth their older FF1 versions had and I recognized this as a misstep, resulting in Frenemies 2. Written initially in 2019, was to reign it in big time, to try to be raw, even bittersweet. In execution however, we have set-ups with no pay offs for events, some characters that could have been delved into but aren't, and still some pacing problems that not even the edits done later could totally fix.
  • For Redux I go all out with the ideal, taking the bits that worked from the previous tales and dropping what didn't. I don't overly shy away from references, new characters, etc, yet I make sure whatever happens is primarily to have plot relevance. I have Mario and Bowser's relationship a friendship and/or community divider like in FF1. I borrow the social commentary, zany OCs, cosmic villainy, and artifacts from New Frenemy Adventure without it requiring lots of backstory or overrunning the show. I make the Mushroom Flu an epidemic with a scandalous past that ties Peach and Bowser together, and use a simplified rule set for Dark Stars like in FF2.
  • I like this one a lot, especially with the edits. As I mention in the final chapter, as I do no epilogue here purposefully, I don't explore everything possible, but that's also the thing. I give you just enough. Everything connects, even seemingly unrelated bits, and I'm not afraid to have some of the edgy NFA had. The content warning is higher here than the others, but not without reason. Enjoy.

Goombas were the last to join Bowser's army

THIS GUY! THIS GUY RIGHT HERE!... Oh this isn't Youtube? I don't have to point?...

Summary: Goombas were the last to join Bowser's army. (Fact) This however isn't. (Maybe) Part of my 'experimental prompt' series sorta for 2018, this one-shot is based on the fact that goombas were the last enemy programmed for Super Mario Bros 1. Why? Koopa Troopas were thought to be a little too advanced for a brand new player, so the goombas that greet you first in this famous game were the last! Irony! This also creates, as I touch on in story, the narrative that the goombas were once with the Mushroom Kingdom but rebelled, an angle actual games after SMB1 don't touch on ever. Enjoy this short little story.

Toads are a Genderless Species

To quote the very elegant Ludwig Von Koopa from Koopatv.org, "It's unclear who Toadette is referring to when she says "we".

Summary: An innocuous DNA test from Prof. Gadd reveals that toads never had genders at all...ZOMG? How will Toadette (known for being the most famous Toad female) change science? She can't. But she can take a third option… ONE SHOT

Where to start for this one-shot.. We sorta have another 'Chemistry' here where I'm being topical and bold and somewhat snarky about Nintendo's interpretations, except here there's other experimental elements on display. The continuity is of New Frenemy Adventure, and it's in first person from Toadette's pov with an intentional wham ending. I'm proud of this prompt to this day, even if this didn't pick up traction for reasons not entirely unexpected. Read the legitimately counter culture one-shot here.

Click for more of my observations (may contain spoilers)
  • I thought this was a bit provocative at the time, and I think it's only gotten more so for poking fun at the ideal. Whatever! (Though I did play genderless toads straight in Frenemies Forever Redux without it being comedic- alas that is written by an older 'wisened' [yeah right] me) Now, while I don't care that much about the topic of headcanons and localization differences and Nintendo flip-flopping or whatever, I like how I wrapped this one up without overly taking a stance outside the main character's thoughts and biases. My works page hosts mostly the stories I'm proud of, so why are these rougher stories here instead of Personal Collections? Well, I am proud, sorta, concept wise at least. Click for more of my observations (may contain spoilers)
  • Let's start with Rhapsody. It has all the attributes of first year submissions from me, where I'm throwing out everything and seeing what sticks. I did like where it was trying to go with the conspiracy against Ludwig that crops up, but it's hit or miss, has a lot of real life references I don't like to do now, and understandably for the age (this was the FIRST fan fic I'd done with Koopalings), the characterization is different from later stories. Note that despite pop and dubstep and other popular genres were being jabbed at here, this clearly isn't me slamming what I hate or was annoyed with like Chemistry, but while demonstrating Iggy's insanity (another character trait I'd drop in other tales) I made fun of 'triggering' which appropriately triggered some guest reviewer.   • Back to square one with Ludwig Goes Pop, with a conflict that's more internal than external, though Ludwig still faces some ribbing over his appreciation of less 'sophisticated' music genres. I put in extra backstory, toss in a few minor OCs to complete the picture and give it a happy ending. The only thing I'd change would be to not reference real songs and singers, just a pet peeve of mine. This being 2017 written though, that predates New Frenemy Adventure where I'd start parodying names and such. Anyway, enjoy! Being self contained ages it far better than Rhapsody.

Super Mario Bros Special

These were.. odd times for Mario

Summary: Mario must save the Princess in an alternate universe where the sky is much bluer, Luigi is not his brother, the false Bowsers aren't false, and there's a strange Hudson Soft powerup… (Short stories)

An alternate take on Mario based on the quirky, sorta not that great, but historically still interesting and important Hudson port of SMB1 for computers of the time. It didn't go as far as I wanted and it's of a short story format, but I thought I made it interesting while it lasted. Being that it was written late 2018 to early 2019, it has that down to Earth tone I was adopting and would use for Frenemies Forever 2, rather than being so over the top like my 2018 stories were with their weirdness. Read the five chapters here.

Click for more of my observations (may contain spoilers)
  • I do believe this was written decently, but an entire retelling of SMB wasn't in the cards for me in late 2018- early 19, where I was either starting stories with more provocative ideals or continuing previous ones. While the Sharp X1 port had some interesting differences from the NES classic, they don't translate perfectly into a novelization. At the end of the day, this is a fish out of water story, Mario's introduction to the Mushroom Kingdom, one many other writers have done before. With me liking to be different, I hope you can see now how I fell off from the ideal.

Archived 11/16/23

Old summary for Mario and Bowser: Frenemies Forever Redux when it was being rewritten: This 2019 work was meant to tell the definite twisted tale of Mario and Bowser's frenemy ways and how this affects everyone around them. Like New Frenemy Adventure the start emulates Frenemies Forever yet again I subvert things. The new villain is reused from the above but most of the godlike powers are toned down so as to not have such an unbeatable threat. Many characters return but others don't or are under emphasized to keep the story lean and uncluttered. Emphasis was made on having a plot coherent from start to finish without too much distracting mystery. FF2 remains more mature, but this time instead of by gory or grim details, from the more grounded and dynamic characters going through more mature and grey area situations. The plenty of references return but in a way that doesn't rely on much knowledge of the other stories. Also fittingly for the final story, Mario and Bowser's relationship is set in stone forever- if that's not too much of a spoiler. Linked above is the story, completed though some edits continue into more recently, and the archive below has the internal author notes and trope page.

*Update!* 8/4/21 (a bit wordy, see the TL;DR at bottom)

Hi again, it’s about time I explain what’s happening with The Mario Clash as a musical project. I’ve planned two EPs, “H.A.A.E. ii v.2” and “A Whole New Level”. The process however was.. not so smooth. Now I’m just gonna release the demos so far. Tracks that are my experiments from using new recording techniques and software. If you are unfamiliar, my old recording method was with Garageband on iOS. With no microphone I would use the tablet’s built in mic to record my live amps. Other effects including the drums would come from Garageband and nothing else. That’s some legitimately powerful and useful software but there were issues regardless and I wanted to produce better sounding live music- not necessarily studio recording level but free from the ordeals I dealt with in ‘BooTracker 3000’ (2016), Hand And Arm Exhaustion’ (2018) and very recently ‘Hand And Arm Exhaustion 2’ (2021).

Now to the present time, I bought a Steinberg interface, Reaper for the PC, and a Shure SM57. Problem solved? In ways yes and no, bringing me to the current project. I wanted to rerecord some tracks from HAAE 2 that I just couldn’t get clean or tight enough along with a new ‘A Whole New Level’ EP that would have a new theme and style of song writing.

The bad: I’m still learning the secrets of good production. No obstacle here but to practice and learn more. Also the drums frankly suck. On Garageband I was often playing the drums myself and that software has some good sounding drum samples. This certainly had it’s own issues considering I’m not really a drummer and did it all on a touch screen, but it had advantages of a better sound with no clipping and the freedom to change the feel and tempo on the spot. Now I’m using a free program called Hydrogen drum machine to create the drum tracks and then importing them into Reaper where I record the rest of the instruments. Hydrogen however has some awful stock sounds and not even that many packs to add and improve it. It also takes forever to do program certain things that aren’t strictly on grid. That may not be the programs fault but it is something I miss from Garageband where I was manually controlling the tempo. (Remember, I never quantize- ever!) Reaper has a plethora of VSTs to use but of course some of the exact ones I was fond of in Garageband aren’t there, and substitutions are necessitated. Again not really its fault. The last issue is so small but one that irritates me most. I can’t easily add album artwork like Garageband did. Windows is supposed to have several ways to do so without third-party software but none of them work!

The good: Better recording quality, period. This goes for instruments, vocals, whatever. I can now double track and mix without slowing down my system. (Previously I could only really pan the guitar one side and bass another. Trust me, anything else was too heavy for the iPad and/or turned into mud) I have much better live amps and equipment to work with too.

Well, that sure seems like more bad than good does it? Actually a lot of that bad can be negated by me becoming more experienced with my new tools and finding alternatives, especially regarding drums. Because of that however I’m not ready to seriously release anything just yet. So now I present what I got so far via Google drive. But there is a little more to this still. For more details track by track, see the release way below under ‘H.A.A.E ii v.2/ A Whole New Level (Demo)’

TL;DR: I’m came pretty far with new equipment but still need time to work out the kinks and produce tracks to my new standard of quality.
