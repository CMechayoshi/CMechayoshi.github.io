You can call me C.Mechayoshi. Of course my actual name is plainly on this site if you know where to look.. My primary interests are gaming, reading, writing, music, and technology, or at least highly specific things that generally fall into those categories. Think certain game series, certain book series, some types of music, niche forms of tech or gadgetry, etc...

As far as other projects go, you'll see me work hard to consolidate highly specific information on the internet and speak about whatever else I'm passionate about. That's just how I roll. My principles are as so: Lost material is unacceptable to me, so I try to archive everything, even seemingly unimportant things. I dislike censorship and I dislike the plain and boring in favor of things that might challenge you or even be a little provocative. Don't confuse it with being wanton or insensitive. I can be opinionated, but not a blowhard over it! I am often galvanized to act when there is a need. Example: There wasn't any extensive coverage of the Nintendo Adventure Books so that became by first big project, besides documenting Charvels and Jacksons, where information was dubious and scattered across the internet on old or dying websites. Many of my creative ideas are odd ones I know.

In conclusion, mechas64castles.net was created off of the foundation of my boomaster-amia (Now under Mecha Nintendo Master) blog c. 2014. I had a screenshot series 'Stuff Gamefaqs Says', which reposted funny comments from the message boards, I wrote quick articles regarding weird or funny Mario subjects, sometimes dumped drafts of fan-fiction, gave commentary in general on upcoming games, and interacted with a fanbase during that weirdish WiiU era, where it got a little rocky. In case you are wondering, I left before the 'big tumblr incident', and my move to a dedicated website was all about having a permanent spot on the web and full ownership of what I put out there. You've certainly noticed everything here is a bit 'old school'. My site is sculpted around, well, incompetence sometimes, but also a desire to capture the state of the web during the 00s, where I first discovered that I could log on a computer and browse gaming and hobbyist fan sites of yore.

Fun fact: the name of this site is a slight reference to themushroomkingdom.net!

My gaming history:

Growing up early on I had a Windows 98 computer and played plenty an edutainment game. It could not handle 3D stuff. Around 2003 I visited some relatives who happened to own a jungle green Nintendo 64. I knew about console games as some of my other relatives possessed the at the time new and fancy Sony Playstation 2. That was the ‘cooler’ console to own then, that and the Xbox for the more mature (or ostensibly so) library of games and because it meant you didn’t need a DVD player. About that same year I was gifted a Playstation 1 Slim and a few games. (Un)fortunately I was already hooked on Mario 64 and Donkey Kong 64 as my new favorite franchises from playing with my cousins.

I was convinced I wanted a N64 despite it being outdated by then. Soon I was given a Gameboy Advance SP by relatives. A brochure with it advertised Mario and Luigi SuperStar Saga. This was the first time I discovered Luigi for instance, who I then mistakenly thought was the older brother due to height. I would consider that Gameboy my true entry into Nintendo fandom as it allowed me to play the ports of Super Mario and Donkey Kong Country games. Yes, I was a 'girl gamer' from then on. Soon I acquired a Gamecube and from that point on I was strictly team Nintendo, even despite its more nerdy stigma among 00s peers. I acquired an subscription to Nintendo Power late 2006 and was subscribed until the magazine's cancellation by the way. Even though now I've branched out considerably, I continue to own mostly Nintendo systems and play first party games.
