Nintendo Adventure Books #5: Pipe Down! Guide

Intro: This is the fifth book in the series by Clyde Bosco again. Mario and Luigi track down the princess after a pair of basketball shoes take her away from her own birthday party. Summary and conclusions: This is the first Nintendo Adventure Book I’d purchased specifically to write a guide on, and I’m glad I did. This book has some odd themes put together, music, dancing and basketball! Bizarre, but it works. There aren’t a lot of areas in the book or even enemies really and the Mario bros are on the move a lot, chasing or exploring. Together they made good protagonists and play off of each other. Too bad it’s a linear tale. The structure of this book isn’t the most open, as you have the opening chase that the reader is given an option to halt quickly or continue for a bit and get an important hint. That is quite an important hint, but the book keeps me from harshly criticizing that part for a reason. See below. There is a toll part of the book you can’t avoid, but if you play your cards right you lose nothing that matters. The ending sequence is extended and fun, allowing the reader to see some different events on repeat readings.


That brings me to the puzzles and win condition. A lot puzzles stuck closely to theme of music or sport. For some reason there’s a lot of gear related puzzles that get tricky and logic based puzzles. Very few are chance based. The biggest puzzle flaw to me is the word search lacking a word bank, since this puzzle tells you the only hint for an item you need. Item checks matter!! The book allows for you to go back however and obtain an item at the expense of getting an inventory wipe. The adventure not give out coins like crazy, so if going for a high score you must keep on it. On one wrong path you can still get by but with a penalty of your coins for instance. I consider than a good design, since there’s an item at the start intended to be used up and has no other value. Hazards in general are done well here, a proper incentive to play well enough to avoid them. Many pages have a lot of paths to them as you’ll notice in the guide, showing that you can jump around and see different things down to the end. I also liked how even on some bad paths instead of sending you straight to the game over, you get a unique leading page before the bad news.


Ludwig, the musical koopaling as a villain is entertaining. His main motivation is evil and petty, just like something he’d do. It’s not exactly a mystery that he’s the bad guy, but they tried to foreshadow it anyway with the music. His pipe organ also plays a key role at the end. He can be menacing and brutal on some pages if you make some bad choices, so be careful. If only there wasn’t a fatal flaw in the scoring of the book. As you can see on the score card linked at top, the threshold for the high score is gained just from winning the book. That’s too bad but who really cares about the high scores anyway? So, linear but well written, weird in the right ways, decently structured, and entertaining. One of my favorites. 5/5

Your score will be five hundred seventy coins for a score of five-thousand five hundred and seventy “Supercharged Koopa-Catching Power Plumber”, far above the top score.


Some explanations of my path:

  • The basketball is never used because we know which way to go at one point, same for the starman which goes unused because we avoid the ‘worser’ path that requires it.

  • It is impossible to not lose an item or not be ‘buffeted’ in this book. You will have to give the clawgrip something on page one-hundred fourteen whether it’s the otherwise useless party favors, boots, or money. On related note you cannot hold all the items in the book at the same time for a couple of reasons. Resetting wipes inventory, and you still miss out on whatever opening puzzle reward you didn’t get.

  • We also cut short the desert chase with the princess because it adds time and has no rewards. More importantly we know the hint. The book on the score page still encourages this however because it leads to said important hint.

  • Balloon: page 1


Puzzle analysis: There are nineteen puzzles in the book. Five are ‘logic’, four ‘decipher’, two ‘get a ruler’, two ‘matching’, one decipher/math, one ‘word search’, one maze/logic, one ‘maze’, one ‘get a ruler’/decipher, one other type puzzle. As mentioned before the puzzles stick to themes well overall, but fall victim to a few issues. Some need some more clear instructions or other courtesies like a word bank. Other than that it’s solid.



*New* Book goals:


Did you visit the desert: this is how you actually track down Peach, not skip any story, and know what boots to use


Find the starman: This item can save you on some pages, but as you see our optimal path doesn’t need it because we make no mistakes. That’s all the hints we get.
