Intro: This is the fifth book in the series by Clyde Bosco again. Mario and Luigi track down the princess after a pair of basketball shoes take her away from her own birthday party. Conclusions: This is the first Nintendo Adventure Book I’d purchased just to write a guide on (as opposed to the others which I acquired for my personal collection) and I’m glad I did. This book has two themes put together for some reason, music or dancing and basketball! The structure is as so: There aren’t a lot of areas in the book or even enemies and while the Mario bros are on the move the entire time, it doesn’t really feel like they travel too much. Expect lots of running in drains too. Basically you have the opening chase that the reader is given an option to halt quickly or continue for a bit and get a hint. There is a toll part of the book you can’t avoid but if you play your cards right you lose nothing that matters. The ending sequence goes on for a while but is fun and doesn’t resolve into linearity as some Adventure Books do.


That brings me to the puzzles and win condition. A lot puzzles stuck closely to theme, music or sport and also gear related puzzles that get tricky. Very few are chance based. The book allows for you go go back and get the item you need, the cowboy boots, at the expense of getting an inventory wipe. To know you need that, well, you can’t exactly until get to the point where the book tells you and it’s too late aside from the reset route. Peach does tell you on the ‘correct’ path of following her that you want boots and not shoes, but there are three boots to choose from so… This book does not give out coins like crazy invoking some inventory management that’s welcomed because it becomes something that actually matters. On one wrong path you can still get by but with a penalty of a lot of coins. The opening items can be a key to get past a certain point but hazards throughout the book can take them away. Overall well designed, punishments that aren’t too harsh but enough to make you avoid them. Many pages have a lot of paths to them as you’ll notice in the guide, this is good, showing that you can jump around and see different things down to the end. I also liked how even on some bad paths instead of sending you straight to the game over, you get a unique leading page before the bad news.


Ludwig, the musical koopaling as a villain is entertaining. His main motivation isn’t wanton evil exactly just quirky and very unfortunate if it would work out. I like how the only foreshadowing is music and how his pipe organ plays a key role at the end. He can be a bit menacing and brutal on some pages if you make some bad choices so be careful. If only there wasn’t a fatal flaw in the scoring of the book. As you can see on the score card linked at top, the threshold for the high score is gained just from winning the book. That’s too bad. 5/5
