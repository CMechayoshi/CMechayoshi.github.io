Nintendo Adventure Books 12: Brain Drain map


Intro: This book, the final of the series, is by Matt Wayne. In the twelfth Adventure Book Iggy swaps the brains of those in the Mushroom Kingdom. Now no one is in their right mind. Literally. This is an online adaptation of a guide I hand wrote around 5/9/19. To follow along F3 search the page listed (type out ‘page x’) or simply scroll down (or occasionally up). It’s not perfect but this worked the best for me. Look at the key page on the portal for the terminology that will make up this walk through and all others.


Relevant links: https://www.mariowiki.com/WMUSH . That page features a summary of events at WMUSH and some scans of the puzzles.

Summary: A fun book staring Luigi. Some of the body-switching antics gave me laughs even if not massively explored once you are in the thick of things. Beyond a certain point, you’re just trying to keep Mario and or Peach from making unwise choices. Finally, you are in enemy territory undercover to foil Iggy’s plans. I liked how natural the Super Mario World settings worked with out being a promotional showcase akin to Book#7 Dinosaur Dilemma. The story structure is not too open ended or linear. There aren't really any loop back and reused pages. You are always guided towards the end, with different ways to get to the goal as long as you are prepared for a late item check, or ‘quiz’ more like, but see below.

Optimal path: START 1- 40- 96 (50pts)- 43- 55 (get tape)- 29 82 (establish secret oath) 119- 19( 70pts*) 109- 51 (50pts)- 33 (50pts)- 49 (50pts)- 87- 103- 102- 64 (50pts, pizza)- 5- 112 (100 pts)- 10- 7 - 26 (100 pts) -78- 62 (100pts) -107- 17- 46- 36 END

Score: 620, “Super Stream-of-Consciousness”. I’m not sure how high you could go because you're forced to not score well at one point for a certain path. As mentioned earlier I couldn't max more than seventy points anyway.

So this route uses one in a half items, the tape to play at WMUSH and the oath which is not credited on the score card. We skip the wrench. Not needed on that path. The pizza is gained only for the points as we avoid the path that requires Luigi to plug his ears to save time. Yeah, yuck, I know. Now here's an interesting thing. On page nineteen you need lesser points to go to page one-hundred nine, which is great because I kept trying for a while and couldn't get high enough for the other path. I don't have it listed as an error because it could be down to my ineptitude. The code you need for the subliminal message puzzle can theoretically be skipped and the book even states ‘IF Luigi has the code’, acknowledging that you might have missed the hint. Worse come to worse, you have a fifty/fifty shot at guessing which isn't really that bad. Alternate paths can be taken here or there which reflects well on the book overall.

Concluding thoughts: This was my favorite as a child. Something about it captured me, and the splits in the book allowed for some different experiences on various reads. I like the characters also getting a look into Koopa HQ . Now, the mystery is obvious, and several puzzles are of the pick a path sort with no definitive correct answer, but they do span different puzzle ‘genres’ as you see in my analysis below and it’d rate them decently. There are no trap ‘items of doom’ unless we count the character Dr. Shrinkasaurus, ut he’s not an item so, doesn’t count! Still, be careful on his pages. For some reason everyone trusts that quack..

Flaws are a few dumb moments in-story and a crucial item almost buried at the start, not hinted at ahead of time, and with zero options to obtain it again. The tape situation is the only fatal flaw here. Still an old favorite as kid, and a pleasurable read even now. 4/5

Item locations:

  • Wrench: page 61

  • Tape/ cassette: page 55

  • Paper/ plumber’s oath: page 82

Puzzle analysis: We have twenty puzzles here, quite a lot and they are eclectic at that. There are five ‘maze’ puzzles, three logic/math puzzles, one decipher, one math/decipher, one math, one spot the difference, one ‘get a ruler’, two word search, two logic/decipher, one logic, and one math/maze puzzle. They aren’t brainless and some take some time to work out.



*New* Book goals: Do you meet Morton? Remember that he’s in toads body and he only attempts to lure Luigi in Peach’s body on ninety-eight. Assuming you tell the truth you will gain some points.

Did you use the phone in the koopa base? It gives the pizza and a bunch of points.

Did you dance to Iggy’s music video? This doesn’t give points, rather just a hint to what should be obvious at that point.

Lastly, there’s no mega mole layer to visit. I checked. Other than that these hints are decently relevant.
